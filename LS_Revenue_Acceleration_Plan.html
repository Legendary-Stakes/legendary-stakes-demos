The Ignition Layer

Phase 2
The Enrollment Push
Days 30–90
Convert Top Sires

Once the dashboards are live, the enrollment pitch practically writes itself. Walk into a meeting with a stallion owner, pull up their sire dashboard on your phone, and show them the royalties their dam owners and breeders are not receiving because their stallion is not enrolled. No deck needed. Just the platform working. Dam owners will begin pressuring sire owners to enroll because enrollment is the only thing standing between them and their royalty checks. That pressure is free marketing.

Phase 3
Entry Fee Flywheel
Days 60–180
Drive Recurring Revenue

Entry fees are the recurring engine that funds everything else. The platform captures 20% of each $250 entry — $50 per entry. An active offspring competing in four categories across 20 events generates $4,000 in annual platform entry fee revenue from a single horse. The projected purse showcase (what would have been paid out at 100% historical participation) is a major activation tool. Build that into the leaderboard experience prominently. The FOMO design does the heavy lifting — you are not just showing competitors what they won, you are showing them what they left on the table.

  • 😤
    FOMO display on every non-enrolled profile: royalties missed by sire owner, dam owner, and breeder based on historical data.
Phase 4
Sponsorship & Advertising
Days 90–150
Layer in Brand Revenue

You do not need millions of users to start this conversation. You need a compelling story about the audience. Once leaderboards are live with active users engaging with horse profiles, you have a monetizable audience. Start with one anchor sponsor on the leaderboard itself — a single national brand like Wrangler, Purina, or a major trailer manufacturer will see immediate value. That deal validates the model and creates social proof for every subsequent sponsor conversation. The vendor marketplace scales with the event network — one or two events integrated with a sponsored banner is enough to prove the model to a brand partner.

  • 1
